How does always allowing public members of an organization work?

If an organization's username is added to the always allowed list, then members of this organization will not be required to sign the CLA. Note that private memberships will not be detected because the service queries the public members of the organization.

How is this different from the other "always allowed" setting?

The "usernames to be always allowed" setting also accepts organization usernames; however that setting only applies to pull requests whose branch belongs to the specified organization. By contrast, the "public members" setting applies to any pull request, including branches from personal forks and local branches.

Why always allow an organization?

Here's two common use cases for this feature:
  • Exempting your own members: If an organization requires all of its members to sign employment contracts or other agreements that supersede the CLA terms, then the organization's own members should not be required to sign a CLA. This works best when memberships are removed after the contracts terminate; for example when an employee leaves the company, removing their membership will ensure that they sign the CLA before making further contributions.
  • A corporate agreement: You receive contributions from another company which has signed a contract that covers contributions from all of its members, and those members submit pull requests using personal forks.
